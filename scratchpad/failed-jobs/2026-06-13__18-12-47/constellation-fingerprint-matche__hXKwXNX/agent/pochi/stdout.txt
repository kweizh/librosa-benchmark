## How it works

1. **Audio loading** — both WAV files are loaded with `librosa.load` at 22 050 Hz.
2. **Log-magnitude STFT** — `librosa.stft` → `librosa.amplitude_to_db` produces a (1025 × N_frames) spectrogram for each signal.
3. **Constellation map** — `scipy.ndimage.maximum_filter` over a 15 × 15 (freq × time) neighbourhood identifies local spectral maxima above −60 dB.
4. **Combinatorial hashing** — for every anchor peak `(t1, f1)`, up to 15 target peaks `(t2, f2)` within a forward window of 1–100 frames and ±64 frequency bins produce the hash key `(f1, f2, dt)`.
5. **Offset voting** — each matching query hash votes for `offset = ref_t1 − query_t1`; the bin with the most votes wins.
6. **Output** — the winning offset is converted to seconds and serialised to JSON.
