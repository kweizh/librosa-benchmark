## What the script does

1. **Loads** each WAV under `/workspace/inputs/` with `librosa.load(sr=None, mono=False)` — preserving native sample rate and channel layout.
2. **Computes global RMS** by flattening all channels and all samples into one vector and taking `sqrt(mean(x²))`, giving a single whole-file amplitude metric.
3. **Calculates ideal gain** = `0.1 / orig_rms` (the factor that would land the file at −20 dBFS).
4. **Clipping guard**: if `peak × ideal_gain > 1.0`, the gain is clamped to `1.0 / peak` so the loudest sample hits exactly 1.0 rather than clipping.
5. **Writes output** with `soundfile.write` (PCM_16), transposing multi-channel audio from librosa's channels-first layout to soundfile's channels-last layout.
6. **Emits `/workspace/report.json`** with `orig_rms_db`, `gain_db`, `final_rms_db`, and `peak_after` for every file.
