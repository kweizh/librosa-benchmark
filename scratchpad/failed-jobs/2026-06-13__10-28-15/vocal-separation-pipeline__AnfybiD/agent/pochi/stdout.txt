The separation pipeline is implemented in `/workspace/separate.py` and has produced both output files.

**How it works:**

1. **Load** — `librosa.load` reads `/workspace/input.wav` preserving the original sample rate and channel layout.
2. **STFT** — a 2048-point STFT (hop 512, Hann window) converts each channel to the complex time-frequency domain.
3. **REPET-SIM background estimate** — `librosa.decompose.nn_filter` with cosine distance and median aggregation finds, for every time frame, the most-similar neighbouring frames and returns their median magnitude. This captures the *repeating* (accompaniment) structure while leaving transient/unique (vocal) content behind.
4. **Soft spectral mask** — `librosa.util.softmask` (power=2, margin=3) derives smooth background and foreground masks that sum to 1 everywhere, so that `fg_mask + bg_mask ≡ 1`.
5. **Inverse STFT** — each masked complex spectrum is inverted back to a waveform with `librosa.istft(..., length=n_samples)`, guaranteeing exact-length outputs.
6. **Write** — `soundfile.write` saves the results at the original sample rate.
