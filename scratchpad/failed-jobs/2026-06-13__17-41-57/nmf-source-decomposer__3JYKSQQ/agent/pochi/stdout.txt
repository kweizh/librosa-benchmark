### Method Overview
1. **Audio Loading**: Loaded `/workspace/mixture.wav` at its native sample rate of `22050 Hz` (length of `220500` samples).
2. **STFT Computation**: Computed the Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) of the mixture using `n_fft=2048` and `hop_length=512`.
3. **NMF Decomposition**: Decomposed the magnitude spectrogram of the mixture into 4 components using `librosa.decompose.decompose` with sorting by peak frequency.
4. **Wiener-Style Soft Masking**:
   - For each component $k$, the magnitude spectrogram was computed as $S_k = W_k H_k$.
   - The soft mask was built as:
     $$M_k = \frac{S_k^2}{\sum_{j=0}^{3} S_j^2}$$
     To ensure absolute robust reconstruction and avoid division-by-zero, we handled the case where the denominator is zero by distributing the mask energy equally ($0.25$ per component). This ensures $\sum_k M_k = 1.0$ everywhere, guaranteeing that the sum of masked complex spectrograms equals the original mixture spectrogram:
     $$\sum_{k=0}^{3} X_k = \sum_{k=0}^{3} M_k X = X \sum_{k=0}^{3} M_k = X$$
5. **Resynthesis**: Applied the masks to the original complex STFT $X$, and performed the Inverse STFT with `length=len(mixture)` to ensure exact length matching.
6. **Feature Computation**: Computed the RMS of each component waveform and the mean of the frame-wise spectral centroids.
